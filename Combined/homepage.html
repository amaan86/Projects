About Us

What We Do

Our vision includes sowing the seeds of positive change in the society which would further lead to the overall growth of the nation, to keep our morals and ethics alive and thereby not letting the humanity die and to uplift the unprivileged thereby maintaining equality and harmony in the society. The major target of Prinkit and his team is to provide education in slum areas. Education being a necessity to lead a fruitful life they identified the problem areas and started taking up projects. According to our Preamble “Any person above the age of seven, knowing any language completely is said to be literate”. Read more

So with the view of making our society capable enough to read, write and calculate their profits and losses, CVC started SLUM EDUCATION CAMPS which are conducted on every Sunday in various areas of Baroda. Slum education includes teaching basic Mathematics and languages include Gujarati and English. Apart from these we+ also teach and try to inculcate basic hygienic habits such as washing hands, bathing regularly, cutting nails weekly, oiling hair and keeping it neat and tied up, etc. In short, to make these people capable enough to compete with the world, CVC started slum education camps.

Many campgains were organized at a small scale but with an increasing volunteer, the campaigns gets a great success. It is because of the continuous efforts of us, that CVC now has more than 300 volunteers who offer their time and support to run our Campaign. Currently including Prinkit, the total numbers of active members who conduct, plan and organise the activities are close to 15. With the help of all such people we have conducted more than 100 slum education camps till now and every Sunday some 200 students join these camps.

We also have taken up various projects highlighting and satisfying our vision like by providing leftover food to slums, CVC stowed its roots towards fulfilling its benevolent vision. For efficient food utilization they identified that they could collect leftover food from parties and marriages. There are many children who suffer from ‘malnutrition’. So with a dream that no person should sleep with an empty stomach and no child suffers from malnutrition CVC took up this initiative. Also they collect old clothes from the stable class of the society and serve it to the needy.

Our Story

First-year engineering student named Prinkit took a small step toward social activity. As he was interested in social activities from his school days. Which makes him to start a small campaign of collecting leftover food from wedding/parties and to distribute to poor people who need food. This campaign was carried out with the help of his some friends. When more of his friends started joining him in those activities, the campaign was named as “Change Vadodara Campaign (CVC) “.

He shared his thoughts with friends and people around which resulted into a bolster from all those minds and hearts, agreeing to his ideology and appreciating his vision. This is how 'Change Vadodara Campaign' abbreviated as 'CVC' emerged. CVC is a group of people having the desire and zeal to help the society with dedicated efforts. Prinkit & his team use social media platform (Facebook, Twitter, Whatsapp,Instagram) to promote their campaigns and to inform about the upcoming event of CVC. Currently, they have started these activities in Vadodara, but with a motive to change the nation.
